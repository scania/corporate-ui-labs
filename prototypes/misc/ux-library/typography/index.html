Scania Sans

The typeface that we use in our internal och external communication is called Scania Sans. The typeface comes in a few cuts; Regular, Bold, Italic, Headline, Headline Bold as well as Condensed, Condensed Bold and a Condensed Italic versions. The typeface is an important part of how we communicate the Scania brand.

Public Headers

Public headers are used on sites where we communicate with users outside of the Scania Network, for example fleet owners, potential new customers and journalists. Basically, the public headers are stronger and bolder than what we use for internal applications. They are most often written in uppercase and the headers should be short, yet still descriptive. Most headers for external use are center aligned.

Article Headers

Headers within the HTML article element should be used for traditionall documents like reports and news articles. The headers are smaller than our other headers to balance better with a smaller bread text. Basically, they are styled for high readability and to work good in print.

Stylesheets

<link rel="stylesheet" type="text/css" href="https://static.scania.com/resources/fonts/scania-sans/scania-sans.css">

The Scania Sans stylesheet should be linked into your application using the tag above. This ensures us that every user of the font has the latest version of it.

Cyrillic Fonts

All Scania Sans fonts are available in cyrillic versions. They are included in the Corporate UI setup. If needed for applications on a stand alone basis, then include the CSS mentioned above and the cyrillic fonts will be loaded when needed.
