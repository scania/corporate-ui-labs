Tablet simplifies training
Global News: 18 Aug 2014
To assist driver its trainers, Scania has developed an innovative tablet application that that replicates all data from the instrument panel. The new WICkit – Wireless Instrument Cluster – includes a durable tablet and a recently-developed device which wirelessly relays data from the control system and transfers information to the tablet.

Training is normally conducted in three stages. Initially, the driver drives the vehicle as he or she normally would. This is followed by a theory session with a focus on economical driving. During the concluding session on the road, the driver receives advice from the driver trainer on how driving habits can be improved.

 

Following the driving session, the trainer and driver can review results.

 

On the tablet, the driver trainer can see real time information from the RPM meter and speedometer that is also supplemented by digital figures. Additionally, the momentary fuel consumption is displayed. With the naked eye its can be difficult to determine the precise pressure on the accelerator pedal but the tablet digitally shows this value as a percentage.

 

The driver trainer also receives information about the number of gear shifts and braking, coasting as a percentage and in kilometres as well as the total distance and total time spent in driver training. Following the session, the instructor can automatically generate a report with accumulated data during training.

 

Following the driving session, the trainer and driver can review results. This report can then be emailed to the driver and his or her employer. The information can also be stored and used during subsequent driver coaching.

 

The instrument cluster is replicated on the tablet screen.
